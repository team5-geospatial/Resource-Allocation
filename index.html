GROUND WATER DEPTH MAPPING

Seasonal Variability:

High groundwater areas remain relatively stable but shift slightly based on seasonal changes, ensuring crucial resources for agriculture and development. Moderate and low groundwater regions display more significant seasonal variation, impacting water availability and management needs.

Resource Planning:

High and moderate groundwater regions can be prioritized for agricultural and development projects. Low groundwater areas need conservation measures and supplementary water supply strategies.

Strategic Importance:

Northeastern regions consistently show high groundwater depth, highlighting their potential as a sustainable water source. Western and southern areas are vulnerable to water stress, requiring focused management before the onset of monsoon rains.

This detailed seasonal analysis aids in informed water resource management and sustainable development planning in Paschim Medinipur.

This map illustrates the groundwater potential zones within the Kangsabati Upper River Basin, categorized into five classes: Excellent, Good, Moderate, Fair, and Poor. The classification is based on hydrological and geospatial parameters, offering insights into the spatial distribution of groundwater resources.

Excellent Zones (Red):
Highest groundwater availability, ideal for resource utilization.
Good Zones (Orange):
Significant groundwater potential, suitable for agriculture and domestic use.
Moderate Zones (Yellow):
Adequate availability, requiring sustainable management.
Fair and Poor Zones (Green):
Limited groundwater potential, highlighting conservation needs.
This analysis aids policymakers, researchers, and planners in making informed decisions for sustainable water resource management in the basin.
